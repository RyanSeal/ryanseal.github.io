Coral Reefs

Coral reefs are diverse underwater ecosystems held together by calcium carbonate structures screted by corals. They are built by colonies of tiny marine life that contain certain nutrients. Most reefs consist of polyps that cluster in groups.

Benefits of Coral Reefs

First off a coral reef can be a source of food for millions of different inderwater life. They serve as guardian as they protect the coastline from storms and any kind of erosion. They provide a habitat for vaurious organisms that share the ocean as a home. They also act as a sort of nursery home for marine as it provides many different nutients and minerals beneficial for marne life.

Coral Bleaching

Coral bleaching happens when a Coral reef suffers a loss of the endosymbiotic algae found in coral. This can happen from warmer then average water temperatures and from certain chemicals from pollution. These factors can cause the reefs an extreme amount of stress that it will expel the algae form the tissue of the reef which in turn makes them a snow white. After bleaching occurs there is a little to no chance of the reef being revived.

Protecting Coral Reefs

There are a few ways you can protect the coral reefs,

1. Conserve water- the less wtaer you use the better. Less water being used the less runoff and waste water will pollute the oceans.

2. Help reduce pollution- Walk, bike, or ride a bus. Various differemt fuel emissions lead to ocean warming which in turn can lead to a lack of sunlight and can ultimatley cause a mass bleaching of corals

3. Dispose of trash properly- Never leave unwanted fishing equipmet such as nets, fishing poles, or various trash from a fishing trip out on the beach. Any and all kinds of litter can pollute the water and cause harm to not only reefs but fish as well.

4. Pactice safe diving and snorkeling- When diving or snorkling please avoid touching or stopping/amchoring the boat over a reef. These actions can damge the reefs.
